The Architectural Evolution: From Scratch to LLM Backbones

The fundamental design of VLMs has undergone a significant transformation. Early models were built from the ground up, requiring extensive training for both vision and language components. The modern approach leverages the immense power of pre-trained Large Language Models (LLMs), focusing instead on efficiently connecting visual data to these powerful text-based backbones.

VLM Benchmark Categories

Research evaluation has diversified into numerous specialized areas. While general visual reasoning remains the largest category, significant effort is now focused on critical issues like hallucination and the application of VLMs in robotics and agent-based systems.

Evaluation Formats in Benchmarks

For ease of automated scoring, the vast majority of benchmarks rely on simple, structured formats. Nearly 45% use a Yes/No format, with multiple-choice questions also being extremely common. This highlights a potential gap in evaluating the nuanced, open-ended reasoning capabilities of advanced VLMs.

Core Challenges Facing Modern VLMs

Despite their rapid advancements, VLMs grapple with fundamental issues that are the focus of ongoing research. These challenges must be overcome to ensure the reliable, ethical, and efficient deployment of this technology.
